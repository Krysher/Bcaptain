About Block Captin

Througout the city of Philadelphia, neighborhoods are organized around a network of block captins (1-2 per city block). There are also often several community organizations serving sections of the neighborhood. Block captins are residents who has taken on the role of information dissemination of their block. They organize clean up activities, share information about events in the area, and hold meetings along with other services. Neiborhood groups sometimes use them to get information to residents.

The Point Breeze area of Philadelphia is the home of approximately 84,000 residents. Within this area, there are between 250 to 300 individual blocks, each having 1 or 2 block captin. Overall coordination is currently disjointed and managed by several folks who are using pen and paper to track which captin belong to each block. The information is currently difficult to maintain and getting information to the captins is not efficient or effective.
